ABOUT ME

Hi, I’m Rania.

I am a Computer Engineering student who enjoys turning complex ideas into clear, useful software and prototypes.

RA / 2026British Columbia, Canada
01MY STORY

I build to understand, and I understand by building.

I’m currently studying Computer Engineering at UBC Okanagan. My work sits at the intersection of software engineering, algorithms, interactive systems, and early embedded prototypes. I am especially drawn to projects where thoughtful technical choices lead to a better experience for the person using the product.

What keeps me interested is the translation process: taking an idea that is still rough, deciding what it needs to do, creating a solid first version, testing it, and then making it clearer and more reliable. That is why I enjoy building tools such as algorithm visualizers, practical workflow applications, browser-based systems, and small systems projects.

02HOW I WORK
01

Start with the problem

I first identify the person, workflow, or concept a project should serve. A good feature needs a real reason to exist.

02

Build a reliable core

I focus on getting the core logic clear and working before adding polish, so the design has a stable foundation.

03

Refine what people see

Once the system works, I improve the experience: the flow, labels, visual hierarchy, and small details that make a tool easier to use.
